Equipment

I like traveling comfortably. As the pannier cyclists say - on hard. It hardly means that I take a lot of equipment with me, regardless of how much it all weighs. This is done at the expense of the kilometers traveled. But I don't care. Some pannier cyclists do not like this type of travel. They prefer minimalism. I prefer my style. I prefer to sleep more comfortably and have most of these things that you normally have at home.
For example, I take with me a heavy self-inflating mat that makes me sleep like I do at home.
You can read about my main equipment items below. These are things proven in long, three-week trips. And you have to take my word for it, but these things have never let me down. And I can't imagine exchanging them for other ones. For me, they are the most important. You have to think carefully when choosing them. The rest of the things are also important, but you can compromise when choosing them.

Bike

I have an old, worn-out Fahrrad Manufaktur. A good bike that has endured a lot and will endure a lot.
Fahrrad Manufaktur are handmade bikes that were created for one purpose: Expeditions.
But the most important part of a bike is the saddle. My saddle is a real work of art. BROOKS saddles have been produced in an unchanged form for over a hundred years. Riding on it, we feel as if we are sitting on the couch.

Tent

Tent this is a second main item. I like having a lot of space in the tent. And most of all, I need the bike tucked inside. That's why I use HUBBA TOUR II tent. It is a two-person tent. Provides plenty of space inside. Moreover, I can hide my bike in the vestibule of the tent. I don't have to worry that someone will steal it from me at night.


HUBBA TOUR II tent. Overnight on the beach in Piaski. On the Vistula Spit.


Sleeping mat

This is the third main item. I use the Therm-a-Rest Trail Pro Large. The dimensions of the mat are impressive: 194 cm x 64 cm. But the most important thing is the thickness of 7.6 cm! The mat is heavy (1.11 kg). But it's incredibly comfortable.
It is really unbelievable that you can sleep in a tent same as at home. Almost.


Therm-a-Rest Trail Pro Large. You sleep like in home.


Panniers

The panniers must, first of all, be waterproof.
I use panniers ORTLIEB company.
But it is not necessary. I just don't like paying twice for something. ORTLIEB panniers are expensive, but I know I bought them once and if someone doesn't steal them, I will have them for the rest of my life.

These were the main items of equipment for each pannier. Of course, a very important item is the cyclist's clothing, but there is so much freedom in this topic that it makes no sense to write about it.

Another very important thing is bicycle tires. In this case, I prefer to drive on heavier but reliable tires. These are tires with a Kevlar insert. Since I put them on the rims on my bike, the tubes have never been punctured.

You don't really need to spend a lot of money to be able to ride a bike with panniers.
I have my saying: "Money does not ride".

Finally, a few tips on equipment.

  • Do not spare on the main things such as a tent or a sleeping mat. I can assure you that they will repay you during the trips, ensuring you safety and comfort.

  • A bicycle does not have to be expensive at all. Can be used. It is important that it is intended for the expeditions. Do not buy other types of bicycles, which are only customized to expeditions. It's good if the bike has a steel, chrome-molybdenum frame. It is very durable and at the same time it damps vibrations very well. A front shock absorber is not necessarily a good option. If anything, the shock absorber must have a blockade.

  • Always take a headlamp with you. It is invaluable at night inside and outside the tent.

  • Always take two sets of underwear and outer clothes with you. On a long trip, there will always be a day when you are driving in the rain. Then everything will be soaked. The second set of clothes will then be alternated.

  • Always take rainwear lightly with you and rain shoe covers.

  • Always take a spare inner tube with you (I always take two tubes).

  • If you have bought a new saddle, it is a good idea to test it on a longer distance first. Because later on the expedition you can have a troubles.

  • If you have bought a new tent, set it up at home first. Sometimes your first night can happen to you in the dark. And then it will be difficult to pitch a tent that you don't know.
