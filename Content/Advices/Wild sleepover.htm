Wild sleepover

As most of my trips take place without a specific plan, sleeping in the wild is inevitable. However, this happens very rarely. Because there is always an agritourism farm on the way, or a campsite where I could pitch a tent. I also like to stay in a guesthouse from time to time to get enough sleep in civilized conditions and wash my clothes. In general, I consider sleeping in the wild as a last resort. But it does happen when traveling by bike. And then what?

There is nothing to be afraid of. It is generally safe in Poland, and the worst animal to be afraid of is ... a tick.
Well, unless we sleep in the Bieszczady Mountains, then the bear can come to our tent. But in Bieszczady Mountains I haven't slept yet, and I haven't had the opportunity to meet him. However, it should be remembered that sleeping in the wild is not allowed in Poland.
My first night in the wild took place on the beach. Of course, this was not allowed and there was a thrill.
During the first night, sleeping in the wild is not very comfortable. Every sound is heard. And most of all, you listen to everything. In my case, the sound of the sea helped a little bit, which drowned out most of the sounds, but I still listened most of the night. In fact, the first night I did not sleep, because it was only a few hours nap. But it was worth it because I watched the sunrise on the beach.

And then, after another short nap, when I opened the tent, I saw a beautiful sight. It was worth a bit of stress for such views.

I do not recommend sleeping on the beach to anyone. It is really not allowed in Poland. The proximity to the border with Russia also made me uncomfortable. After all, a border patrol might appear at any moment. And then there wouldn't be too long negotiations. I would just get a mandate.

 

During these few nights of mine, sleeping in the wild, I came to several conclusions. I will not write too much. There are tons of guides on the Internet about sleeping in the wild. I will write just a few of my observations.

  1. First of all, a tent. It has to be one that is unobtrusive. I have green. There are also white tents, orange ones. This one will definitely be visible.

  2. You must find a place to sleep in the wild during the day. Sometimes I pitch a tent in the dark. If you pitch a tent on a campsite, it does not matter, but if you pitch a tent in the wild, you must do it during the day. I happened to pitch a tent in the dark once in the wild, and then in the morning it turned out that the place was not very pleasant. But it could have been worse, for example in an anthill or some animal excrements.

  3. Better not ask any people in advance where you can set up your tent, as this will arouse interest and you may have uninvited guests at night.

  4. Remember that sleeping in the wild in Poland is punishable by a fine. Also, in the morning after waking up, you may find that a forest guard will visit you and you will get a mandate. Especially on the beach or the dunes. I slept on the beach, but luckily I didn't get a ticket.

  5. And you know, it's best to pitch a tent as far away from people, houses and roads as possible. Road noise may disturb you at night. And if you put up your tent too close to people and buildings, you may have uninvited guests at night.

 

Sometimes the night may end up even on the floor. Once in Podlasie I had nowhere to stay, and it was already late. Somehow it was hard to find a good place to sleep in the wild. So I asked random people if they would spend the night at their home. They offered me a night in their summer cottage. But there was no equipment in this summer cottage, so I landed on the floor. Paradoxically, I had the best sleep then.

And finally, these are my thoughts. I consider sleeping in the wild as a last resort. When there really is nowhere to sleep. Generally, there are so many different campsites, guesthouses and campsites in Poland that you can always find something for yourself. However, there is always such a possibility that you will have to sleep in the wild. It's good to prepare for it in advance. Even mentally.
