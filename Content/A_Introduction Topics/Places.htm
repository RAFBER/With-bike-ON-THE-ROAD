Places

During my trips, I have visited many interesting places. They were both architectural objects and places where you can eat regional dishes. There are hundreds of them. I have described many of them in the chapter Expeditions. However, in this chapter I would like to show you places that are truly unique in the whole country.

Bridges in Stańczyki

One of my first interesting places I visited in Podlasie were the bridges in Stańczyki. These are two railway bridges built in 1912-1918. The facilities have a reinforced concrete structure, five-span with equal arches, 15 meters high. They are twins to each other. Architects from Italy designed them.

The bridges are located in the Romincka Primeval Forest in the land called "Mazury Garbate". It is a very picturesque land that I had the opportunity to travel by bike. The terrain is very undulating. There are the highest hills in northern Poland. The highest mountain in the region is Mount Rowelska, 298 m above sea level high, which I had the opportunity to ride my bike on.

I have read that the pillars are decorated with elements modeled on the Roman aqueducts in Pont du Gard. That is why both railway bridges are often called "Aqueducts of the Romincka Forest".

The bridges in Stańczyki are among the highest bridges of this type in Poland. They are 200 m long and 36 m high.

Coordinates: 54°17′50,8″N 22°39′16,6″E

The Śliwno-Waniewo bicycle bridge

It is a system of platforms and pontoon platforms located on the Narew River and its backwaters. The Śliwno-Waniewo bicycle bridge is located in the Narew National Park, in the Podlaskie Voivodeship. The footbridge is used to cross the town of Śliwno and Waniewo. It consists of fixed platforms as well as five pontoon platforms which are moved with the help of the strength of the muscles. The entire crossing is 1250 m long. During it, we can admire the beauty of the Narew River backwaters. Unfortunately, the low water level in Poland in recent years means that the footbridge is often closed.

I was also unlucky that when I got there, the footbridge was closed, and I could only get to half of the pool. But it was still worth it. The views were unforgettable.

There are observation towers along the footbridge from which you can observe the backwaters of the Narew river. I left this place a bit disappointed that it was impossible to get across the whole pool. But it was worth making up for the kilometers anyway.

Podlasie Bar in Białystok

There is a place in Bialystok where time stopped some 40 years ago. This place is a Podlasie bar, popularly known as Podlasiak. The bar is located in the very center of Białystok at Kościuszko Square.

The bar is self-service. During your stay there, you can feel like in a Stanisław Bareja film. First you have to take a tray and ask the lady for soup, main course, salads and compote. And then you have to go to the cashier with that tray. The lady at the counter, dressed in an old, white apron as it used to be. And a white bonnet on his head.

When I was there, the lady at the cash register was eating guts between one customer and the next. So sometimes a few spoons, and other customer, and then another, and then a few spoons again. Comedy.

And the food? Well ... It's not top level. Such typical, bar food.

Unfortunately, I was not allowed to take pictures inside the bar. I just surreptitiously took a picture of the price list. Such price lists can only be seen in old movies. Such prices are nowhere to be seen. Broth with noodles - 2.71 PLN, pork chop - 9.41 PLN ...

But it's worth going there if you like the atmosphere of Stanisław Bareja's films, of course.

 

Orthodox church of the Protection of the Mother of God in the village of Puchły.

During my trip to Podlasie in 2019, I saw many beautiful Orthodox churches. I showed many of them in the pictures in the Expeditions section. However, one of them deserves special attention. It is the Church of the Protection of the Mother of God in the village of Puchły. The church was built in 1913, and it is really beautiful.

I was lucky that there was a priest in the church. But a priest in the Orthodox Church is called Batiushka.

Batiushka let me in, showed me the interior of this beautiful Orthodox church and told various interesting stories about the Orthodox Church in Poland.

I was going to this church for a few kilometers of rain, but it was really worth seeing.

Moving dunes near Łeba

There is a place in Poland where you can feel like you are in a desert. This place is the moving dunes near the city of Łeba. Moving dunes located about 7 km from the city center, in the Słowiński National Park. To get to the dunes, you need to get to the car park in Rąbka, which is located 2.5 km from the center of Łeba. Then walk 4.5 km to the dunes. You can also rent a bike in Łeba.

The highest dunes are 30-40 meters high and move several meters a year.

The highest dune is the Łącka dune and its height is about 40 meters. After entering the dune, there is a beautiful view of the Łebsko Lake and the Baltic Sea in the same time.

Coordinates of the parking lot in Rąbka: 54°45′14″N 17°30′58″E

The small fishing port of Unieście

During the Eurovelo R10 route in 2020, I passed a lot of ports, fish bars, fish smokehouses. But one port in particular stuck in my memory. There is a small fishing port Unieście near the town of Mielno in the West Pomeranian woivodeship. The port is located between the Baltic Sea and Lake Jamno.

There are several fish smokehouses and stalls on the promenade of the port where you can buy fresh fish. There are also several fish fryers. The fish I had there was really delicious. I couldn't deny myself the warm eel that I love.

The eel came from the Szczecin Lagoon and was the best I have eaten in my life. It is a place that I will definitely go to again someday.

Coordinates: 54°16′16″N 16°05′52″E

 

Gosań - the highest cliff in Poland

When I was cycling to Świnoujście on route 102, I came across Gosań by coincidence. Klif Gosań is the highest cliff in Poland. Its height is 93.4 m.
To get to it, I had to leave my bike downstairs in front of the hill and walk 100 m on foot uphill. I was a bit scared for the bike, but the landscape I saw after climbing the cliff made up for my fears. It was really beautiful there.

It was by far the most beautiful view on the Polish coast that I have ever seen. The view of the Baltic Sea from the height of 93 m is truly captivating.

If you are going by car, you have to leave the car in the parking lot by the road, and then walk a few hundred meters.

Coordinates: 53°57′12″N 14°29′09″E

 

Benedictine monastery in Mogilno

In the city of Mogilno, in the Kuyavian-Pomeranian voivodeship, there is a Benedictine monastery dating back to 1096. The monastery welcomes tourists, and you can spend the night in it. When I'm around, I'm always happy to go there. Sometimes it is worth choosing something like this instead of a guesthouse, hotel or other agritourism.

The atmosphere is unique and the view from the window of your room is amazing. The lake that you can see outside your room window is the Mogileńskie Lake.

The monastery is located on a hill above the Mogileńskie Lake.

Sometimes there were days when only I slept in the whole monastery, and there was no one else there.

The oldest well in Poland is located in the cloister's courtyard.

Currently, the monastery is ruled by the Order of Friars Minor Capuchins.

Andrzej Seweryn made his first film in the monastery as a director. The film was entitled "Who Never Lived".
