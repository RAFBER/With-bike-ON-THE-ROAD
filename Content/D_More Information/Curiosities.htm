Curiosities

During my trips, I come across all sorts of curiosities. In principle, they could be placed in the Places and Expeditions sections, but they are so remarkable that it is worth listing them in the Curiosities section.

Crooked Forest

What is this? A natural monument with an area of approx. 0.33 ha, located in the vicinity of the new housing estate of Nowe Czarnowo village and Dolna Odra power plant, in the Gryfino poviat. The area was so named due to the growing of about 100 deformed Scots pines.

These trees are bent at an angle of approx. 90 ° from approx. 20 cm above the ground, and the curvature of some of them reaches a height of 3 m. Their total height is approx. 11–12 m.

There are several theories about how these trees came about:

  • The best known theory is that the trees were shaped as a result of the deliberate activities of people who cultivated them specially for the so-called cams for carpentry or boatbuilding purposes, such as the construction of furniture, boats, sleds, etc.

  • Another theory is that the reason for the unusual curvature of the trunks was the cutting down of trees aged 6 to 10 years for use in Christmas trees. Because the trees were left with the last lower branch on purpose, it continued to grow vertically and with time took on a characteristic, distorted shape.

I personally like the theory that these trees were shaped by humans in order to make boats, furniture, etc.
I liked the fact that you can just sit on them.

Tatar Mosque in the village of Kruszyniany.

Few people know that there is a Tatar community in Poland. They mainly live in the indigenous Tatar colonies in the villages of Bohoniki and Kruszyniany. However, there are few of them there. About 2 thousand. I decided to visit the village of Kruszyniany and the mosque there, as well as the cemetery. The mosque is wooden and can be visited. If you are there, you must make an appointment with a guide to visit the mosque. Since a mosque is a sacred place, religious orders must be followed. Before entering, take off your shoes. It is a religious order as in any Muslim mosque. It is forbidden to drink alcohol in the mosque or be under the influence of it. In the mosque, it is not allowed to take pictures, make films, etc. I was lucky that the guide was at the mosque. He told me about the Tatar community and how the Tatars found themselves in Poland. He also told me about his family, that his wife is a Christian. An interesting fact is that they have two children and one of them is brought up in the Muslim religion and one in the Christian religion. The stay in the mosque was an amazing experience and if you are in the vicinity of Białystok, you must go there. Near the mosque is also located a Tatar cemetery. In the Tatarska Jurta restaurant near the mosque you can eat traditional Tatar dishes.

Tsarist Road

The Tsarist Road is a 33-kilometer section of the road from the bridge over the Narew River in Strękowa Góra to the intersection with the national road No. 65 in Osowiec-Twierdza in the Podlaskie voivodeship. This is the common nomenclature because in fact the road is much longer.
The road runs through the Biebrza National Park. What's so interesting there? After all, it is theoretically the usual road, but...
On this entire stretch of road, the speed limit is 50 km/h. Not only that, but there are also speed bumps, and you can often meet police patrols.
This is because there are many moose there and if you drive faster, a collision with this animal is very likely.
But the most interesting thing on this path are ... flies. If you are driving a car, you have no problem, but if you are cycling, it is not comfortable there anymore. You must cycle the entire 33-kilometer section at a speed of over 25 km / h. If you are cycling below 25 km / h you have flies in your nose, eyes, ears, everywhere. And they bite. That fly is a bovine bittern. They are 2-2.5 cm long and are everywhere. This fly is often confused with a hornet because it is so big. Anyone who has traveled that way by bike will not forget it for the rest of his life. I turned back after 1 kilometer. I even had flies between my glasses and eyes. It is very dangerous to cycle like this. I will never ride my bike that road again. Interestingly, a friend was riding his bicycle a week earlier and the flies were gone. I have not seen such flies anywhere else in Poland. If I asked other people, no one had heard about this fly. Only one of my colleague, who comes from this region confirmed that such a fly exists.. I also was warned by other cyclists on the phone who had been riding that day earlier.
An unforgettable adventure. Never again.
